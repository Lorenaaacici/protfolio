Project Overview

Brisentinel

"Brisentinel" is a platform that integrates property listings with essential risk assessment data, such as flood zones and crime rates, to help users make informed real estate decisions. The platform features an intuitive interface that allows users to explore properties through an interactive map.

By visualizing key data points, "Brisentinel" aims to enhance user decision-making and prioritize accessibility. This portfolio outlines the design and development process, covering key challenges, iterations, and design principles used to create a user-centered and functional platform.

Design Concept

The design concept focuses on creating an intuitive and user-friendly interface that prioritizes accessibility and interactivity. This section covers the initial inspirations, research, and key design principles considered during the development process.

Our approach prioritizes accessibility, ease of navigation, and visual interactivity. Throughout the development, we focused on key principles such as responsive design, intuitive layouts, and engaging data visualizations. Process & Evidence

4A — Team Agreement Document

Team Progress:

At the start, we focused on creating the initial design concept for "Brisentinel," combining real estate listings with disaster risk data. After reviewing various datasets from the State Library of Queensland (SLQ), we selected key data on floods, bushfires, and crime to be visualized on an interactive map. Our original idea of a disaster alert platform evolved into integrating risk information with property data for more practical use.

Datasets Selected:
  • Flood Data: Flood Awareness Creek Dataset — Awareness — Creek (Brisbane City Council)
  • Crime Data: Crime Locations Dataset — last 5 years (QLD Government)
  • Bushfire Data: Wildfire History Dataset — History (Brisbane City Council)

My Contribution:

  • Research: I led the dataset research and identified flood, crime, and bushfire data for integration. I analyzed these datasets and proposed their use in an interactive map for risk visualization.
  • Design Concept: I contributed to shaping the platform's direction by suggesting the integration of risk data with property listings. This approach shifted the project from disaster alerts to a property-centric focus.
  • Key Functions: I helped define the platform’s main features, including disaster risk mapping and property comparisons, and provided early sketches for how the map would function.

4B—Project Proposal Presentation and Slides

Team Progress:

We worked with three datasets: bushfire, flood, and crime rate. The flood data showed historical occurrences and future likelihoods, while the crime rate data was organized by Brisbane's suburbs. We aimed to combine these datasets to create a comprehensive safety index for property listings.

A major challenge was presenting this data intuitively and interactively. We aimed to create an interface where users could easily explore and understand these risk factors and their impact on property decisions.

Initially, we designed a homepage introducing the platform. Based on feedback, we shifted to displaying the platform's key feature—an interactive map—on the homepage, allowing users to explore safety and property information simultaneously.

My Contribution:

  • Logo Design: Designed the logo representing safety in property selection.
  • Color Palette: Selected a bright color scheme to promote user trust.
  • UI/UX Design: Designed the website’s UI/UX in Figma, focusing on usability.
  • User Personas: Developed user personas to guide design decisions.
  • Storyboard: Created a storyboard to visualize user interaction scenarios.
  • Web Framework: Assisted with building the initial web framework, working on HTML and CSS to implement the design.

4C—MVP Demonstration

Team Progress:

During the MVP phase, we built a functional prototype of "Brisentinel" to showcase core features, including the Risk Map, Property Ranking, and Recommendation Page. These features aimed to provide an interactive and informative user experience by integrating safety data with property listings.

Key challenges included inconsistencies in datasets and designing an effective UI. We addressed slow response times by caching crime data and restructured data integration using the MVC pattern to streamline the approach.

Key achievements included the Risk Map with hover effects for visualizing danger areas, a Property Ranking system to compare suburbs based on safety, and a Recommendation Page that combines property listings with safety metrics using a dynamic layout and animations.

My Contribution:

  • User Testing: Led user testing sessions using the Think Aloud method, gathering valuable feedback on user experience and interaction.
  • Rank Page Content: Gathered and organized content (introduction, images) for the rank page, ensuring it was clear and informative.
  • Logo Redesign: Modified the logo to better align with the platform’s theme and visual identity.

Reflections

1. Design Thinking and Reflection: One of the most important aspects of the course was the emphasis on user-centered design. In every phase, I iterated on feedback from tutors and users to refine our design. Early paper prototyping and user testing exercises were pivotal in understanding how users interact with the platform. This approach helped me focus on error prevention and consistency in user interaction, contributing to a more accessible interface. For instance, based on user feedback, I integrated a clearer flow on the Ranking Page by making the interaction more intuitive. Moving from a disaster alert platform to a more user-focused property risk assessment tool allowed me to address real-world challenges more effectively.

2. Technical Challenges and Solutions: The dynamic components of our project, such as the API integrations and interactive map, presented significant challenges in both implementation and design. Learning how to use PHP to handle data retrieval from APIs taught me how to structure data efficiently. Additionally, the filtering and caching methods, especially Regex for validation, enhanced my understanding of back-end data management, optimizing performance when visualizing complex data like flood and crime rates. For example, choosing PHP for data handling was driven by its robustness and ease of integration with our existing systems, which ultimately improved the user experience by providing faster and more reliable data access.

3. Prototyping and MVP Evaluation: Presenting our Minimum Viable Product (MVP) was a crucial part of our progress. During the MVP demo, I encountered unexpected display issues, emphasizing the importance of testing across different platforms to ensure adaptability and responsiveness. I also realized that despite having a solid conceptual framework, functional presentation plays a key role in delivering the intended experience. User feedback during this phase highlighted the need for a more intuitive navigation system, which led to significant improvements in the final design.

4. Team Collaboration and Roles: The collaboration aspect was another key learning point. We each took on multiple roles—design, research, and coding—ensuring a holistic understanding of the project’s scope. Personally, I led the dataset research and played a major role in defining the platform’s key features. My teammate, Alex, focused on the front-end design, ensuring a seamless user experience, while Jamie handled the back-end integration, making sure our data was efficiently processed and displayed. This multi-disciplinary approach taught me the importance of balancing design aesthetics with functional coding, and the need for clear communication within the team to align our efforts towards a common goal.

Critical Takeaways

Looking back, my growth in interaction design, UX principles, and technical implementation stands out. The practical application of design theories through iterative processes helped me create more functional designs while considering user engagement at every step. Moving forward, I plan to apply these lessons to future projects, ensuring a balance between functionality, usability, and aesthetic quality.
